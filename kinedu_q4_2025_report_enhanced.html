Executive Summary

Q4 2025 showed significant sequential improvement with revenue up 44% QoQ to $1.11M, driven primarily by recovery in December ($445K, +23% MoM). However, the company remains significantly behind budget (-46% in Q4, -44% for full year) and generated a net loss of $96K EBITDA despite the revenue uptick. Full year 2025 closed at $3.53M revenue with -$824K EBITDA and -$897K cash burn, representing substantial underperformance against a $6.34M revenue budget and +$650K EBITDA target.

What drove Q4 performance:

  • Strong December recovery boosted quarterly revenue 44% QoQ after weak Q2-Q3 performance ($771K-$785K range)
  • Marketing spend increased 31% QoQ to $562K, suggesting volume-driven growth strategy rather than efficiency gains
  • Contribution margin improved to $292K (26.3% margin) vs $152K in Q3, indicating better unit economics at higher volumes

Key risks and watch-outs:

  • Structural revenue shortfall: 44% below budget for full year suggests fundamental issues with conversion, retention, or pricing assumptions
  • Negative operating leverage: Increased marketing spend did not translate to profitability—higher volumes still produced losses

Analysis: Conversion strength offset by CAC deterioration and ARPU decline
Q4 showed strong conversion improvement (+63% vs budget at 7.87%), indicating the company is getting better at converting installs to paying customers. However, this was achieved at significantly higher CAC ($1.61 vs $1.26 budget, +28%) and lower ticket sizes ($52.53 vs $57.20, -8.2%). The net effect: estimated LTV:CAC of 16.2x in Q4 vs 20.7x budgeted—still healthy by industry standards, but trending downward. ARPU collapsed from $33 in Q1-Q2 to $26 in Q3-Q4, a 21% decline that suggests either pricing pressure, shift to monthly plans, or higher churn reducing lifetime value.

Strategic Signals & Implications

Data-driven insights for decision-making

1 ARPU recovery is now the critical path—not CAC reduction
The KPI data reveals the core issue: ARPU collapsed from $35 (Jan) to $22-25 (Aug-Oct), a 29-38% decline that directly caused the revenue shortfall. While CAC is 28% above budget in Q4 ($1.61 vs $1.26), conversion improved dramatically (+63% vs budget), meaning the company is acquiring customers efficiently on a unit basis. The problem is each customer generates 15% less revenue than expected ($26 actual vs $30 budget ARPU). At current volumes, every $1 improvement in ARPU adds ~$350K in annual revenue with zero additional marketing spend.
2 Conversion is working—volume assumptions were wrong
Conversion rates exceeded budget by 63% (7.87% actual vs 4.82% budget in Q4), climbing steadily from 3.76% in January to 8.36% in December. Free trial conversion also performed well at 14.72% in Q4. This means the company is extremely effective at converting users who enter the funnel—the problem is not enough users reached the funnel. The 44% revenue miss despite 63% better conversion indicates top-of-funnel volume (installs, traffic) was dramatically overestimated.
3 December performance provides template but sustainability is unproven
December generated $445K revenue (best month of the year) with -$41K EBITDA (best month since April). If this is repeatable, the company could be on track to near-breakeven operations. However, December's strength could be seasonal (holiday gifting, New Year resolutions) or due to one-time marketing campaigns. Early warning signal for Q1 2026: If January revenue falls back below $350K or burn exceeds $60K/month, December was likely an outlier.
4 Operating structure is built for a $6M+ company but only generating $3.5M
Operating costs of $1.57M annually (including $1.04M in salaries) represent a fixed cost base designed for the $6.3M revenue budget, not the $3.5M reality. At current revenue levels, OpEx is 44% of gross sales vs the budgeted 23%. This overhead structure will prevent profitability even if revenue grows 20-30% in 2026. Strategic decision required: Either right-size the team and cost structure to match actual revenue (targeting $1.0-1.2M annual OpEx, requiring ~25-30% cost reduction), or commit to aggressive revenue growth plan to "grow into" the current cost structure.
